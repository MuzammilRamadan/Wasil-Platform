Epidemic Case Reporting

Community members can report disease symptoms and outbreak cases directly from their phones, enabling real-time data aggregation across affected regions.

Live Outbreak Monitoring

A real-time dashboard tracks active outbreaks by area, disease severity, and case count — providing health authorities with actionable situational awareness.

Mobile Clinic Coordination

Health organizations can submit clinic deployment requests to epidemic-hit areas. MOH admins review and approve these deployments for maximum impact.

Health Service Requests

Community members can request vaccinations, ambulances, medical consultations, water sanitation aid, and nutrition support — all in one place.

Role-Based Access Control

Three distinct roles — Community Member, Organization, and MOH Admin — each with tailored dashboards and permissions for secure, efficient operations.

Bilingual Support

Full Arabic and English localization ensures that all communities across Sudan can access health services and information in their preferred language.

Our Story

WASIL was born out of the urgent need to modernize epidemic response coordination in Sudan. During health crises, critical information — where outbreaks are concentrated, which areas need clinics, what services are available — is often fragmented across phone calls, paper records, and disconnected systems. This fragmentation costs lives.

Our team of developers, health professionals, and community advocates came together with a single goal: build a unified digital bridge that connects every stakeholder in the health response chain — from a community member reporting a fever in a rural area, to an NGO deploying a mobile clinic, to a Ministry of Health official monitoring the national epidemic situation.

WASIL is built with Sudan's unique context in mind — mobile-first, Arabic-first, and designed to function effectively even in low-connectivity environments. We are continuously improving the platform based on feedback from the communities we serve.
